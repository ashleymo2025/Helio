More on Helio's Next Generation
Biosensor.

Symptoms of Lupus appear differently in everyone. In order to give an accurate response, a patient’s family history and physical symptoms must be referenced along with the results given by Helio’s program.
Nanosensors have been applied to other areas of the medical field including glucose monitoring, detection of cancer cells, and detection of volatile organic compounds (VOCs) as early biomarkers for a wide range of diseases
Current techniques such as enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA test) are well established and effective but require complex sample labelling processes and long waiting times (3-8 hours). Helio’s diagnostic test would give an accurate response in 30m-1 hour
We’re using aptamer-based nanosensors because of their strong bonding capabilities, high specificity, sensitivity and low cost. Especially when diagnosing lupus it is extremely important to differentiate between normal antibodies and different types of ANA antibodies. This will allow Helio to gather large data sets on each patient and create an accurate diagnosis for lupus.
It would be impossible to find one test to measure all possible lupus biomarkers (as some are in the urine or shown on the skin, etc.). However, the earliest biomarkers and the ones most specific to lupus appear in the blood, where there are still dozens to measure which is why we thought it was an area with a lot of potential for improvement.
