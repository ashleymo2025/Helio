How Misdiagnosis Emerges.

There is no root cause for lupus.

Because its presence is so distinct from person to person, lupus diagnosis is tricky. We have to rely on a combination of very unspecific symptoms to make a final statement.

According to the Lupus Research Alliance, 4 out of 11 common lupus symptoms must be present to a severe degree; if not, up to 7 or 8 different ones are needed to be visible.

For the patients, this causes them to fall into a spiral of never ending biomarker tests, specifically involving the blood (ANA antibodies, presence of any blood disorders, inflammatory proteins, etc.). Ultimately, all of these consequences resulting from the tedious, inaccurate, and costly methods we use to track lupus biomarkers only delay patients from receiving a proper diagnosis, and thus treatment to suppress their painful symptoms.

Months of waiting
  • Not only do the tests need to be taken one at a time, they often require processes that need overnight incubation. It can take a week for a patient to hear back about their results. Additionally, the only way to get tests is through booking an appointment with a rheumatologist which can take up to six months. The wait pushes patients farther from a proper diagnosis
At least $500 of testing
  • One blood test can cost up to $60 USD, so patients need a couple hundred dollars in their pocket, which simply isn’t worth it. Many patients have chosen to drop their case due to the fact that all the individual tests combined are so expensive.
Human error + subjectivity
  • The blood tests are often all carried out manually, which can lead to human error. Also, the aspect of interpreting the test results is based on human observation which opens doors to subjectivity and alas a misdiagnosis. Certain doctors may refuse to provide certain tests, thinking their patients don’t need it, when in fact they were necessary in order to make an accurate diagnosis.
Physically demanding
  • It’s not uncommon for a typical patient to get at least 12 vials of blood extracted from their arm when undergoing the testing process. The back and forth running to get tests and waiting with harmful symptoms is nothing less than a painful experience.
