The Status Quo

Check ins are not frequent

With routine lab tests conducted on average four times per year means there is very little information given about the progression of one's lupus and their responses to various environments and treatment. This turns optimizing care into great challenge.

A reactive approach

Changing medication or medication dosage after or during patients recount their symptoms and flares is reactive vs. proactive. This means there is damage done to the body before changes to treatment are made while ideal treatment should be prescribed in advance to suppress autoimmune responses

Helio

Taking advantage of nanotechnology and machine learning to reform the way lupus is monitored and how patient information is used to create personalized and proactive care.
