Big data

In order to get the most accurate diagnosis, we need to ensure we take as many blood biomarkers we can that could give any indication of the onset of lupus. This involves measuring various antinuclear (ANA) antibodies to blood cell levels to inflammatory proteins. When we combine all this data, we are then able to analyze this data for hidden patterns that could detect the possibility of an individual having lupus.

Nanosensors

Using aptamer-based nanosensors to detect blood biomarkers associated with lupus is cheaper, more accurate and takes less time. This allows us to test for and even differentiate types of ANA antibodies and blood cells within a matter of minutes, and accurately test for the presence of lupus.

Outcome

Diagnosis is possible within a couple minutes versus months or even years. Without needing dozens of separate tests and with a fully automated process, not only is it much faster and cheaper, but it also removes the aspect of human subjectivity, allowing the rest of the 5+ million people with lupus globally to reach proper treatment faster.
