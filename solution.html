What it Takes to Tackle Big Problems. 

Big Data

In order to get the most accurate diagnosis, we need to ensure we take as many blood biomarkers we can that could give any indication of the onset of lupus. This involves measuring various antinuclear (ANA) antibodies to blood cell levels to inflammatory proteins. When we combine all this data, we are then able to analyze this data for hidden patterns that could detect the possibility of an individual having lupus.

Nanosensors

Using aptamer-based nanosensors to detect blood biomarkers associated with lupus is cheaper, more accurate and takes less time. This allows us to test for and even differentiate types of ANA antibodies  blood cells, and proteins within a matter of minutes, and accurately test for the presence of lupus.

Artificial Intelligence 

In the medical diagnostic field, the integration of machine learning is becoming increasingly prominent, being used to predict the possibility of the onset of particular diseases. At Helio, we’re using it to detect unusual patterns in the various blood biomarkers that are different from the average person, and could indicate the presence of lupus better than humans.

Our Technology.

How Helio's Nanosensor will Function

An aptamer-based nanosensor is a combination of biotechnology and nanotechnology. It relies on the recognition properties of a biomaterial (an enzyme, protein or nucleotide) and a specific biomarker. They have attracted much attention due to their great strong bonding capabilities, high specificity, and bio-compatibility.

The nanosensor will include 8-10 wells, where a drop of blood is placed into each with a micropipette. Each well will then go on to detect a particular distinct blood biomarker.

Building the Machine Learning Model

The results from the nanosensor will be fed through an artificial neural network, and will produce a percentage as an output. The algorithm will be trained with data collected from the nanosensor that measured the biomarkers of both lupus and healthy patients and will learn to classify new datasets based on the patterns it has found in its training + self-evaluation.
