Helio's Biosensor

Helio's biosensor is an at-home device where disposable strips are inserted and blood is places upon the strips through a well. There will be 2-3 strips, where each strip will measure a different biomarker. Biomarker quantities from all strips in the device will be measured simultaneously each time the test is taken.

Our biosensor will have strips available for Anti-dsDNA, Anti-NCS, C3, C4, EC4D, and BC4D.

The Learning Phase (3-6 Months)

Biomarker data will be plotted onto a line graph and updated in real time. The app will notify patients after a period of time if it sees patterns between certain weather, patient activity, and mood and high biomarker activity. It will also associate certain types of medication and their dosages with how well they are at maintaining low autoimmune activity.

This can ultimately allow patients to find how sensitive they are to certain surroundings and notify doctors on which medication is ideal to suppress worsening symptoms.

The Flare Forecast

After 3-6 months of incoming data, a flare predictor is added which by this time should have enough individual specific data to understand the nature of lupus in the patient by this time.

Like the conventional weather forecast, we take in loads of data, analyze trends and predict what flares will look like for the next 7 days. Data includes daily biomarker results, logged in patient data, and daily treatment. Everyday of the week will show the likelihood of them having any flare that day and intensity of the possible flare.

How it Works

A six-step breakdown

1

Diagnostic Test

The doctor will perform a diagnostic test to determine which biomarkers (2-3) are suitable to measure disease activity in the patient.

4

Biomarker Results

After 2-5 minutes, results will show on the Helio mobile app, and strips are replaced.

2

Setting up the Biosensor

Disposable strips will be prescribed, which will be put into a hand-held cartridge.

5

Patient Data

Activities and mood will be logged in, while treatment for the week will already be logged in.

3

Using the Biosensor

A drop of blood from the finger will be put into the well of the cartridge.

6

Evaluation

Over time, the app will identify patient triggers and which treatments have the greatest efficacy. After 3-6 months, the app can predict the likelihood of flares.
